# Internal — Sprint Complexity / Points Rubric (Not Client-Facing)

**Purpose:** Keep scope honest while maintaining a clean external promise (2 workflows minimum; 2–4 depending on complexity).

## How to use
- Assign points **per workflow** during Days 1–3.
- Use points to decide whether we can safely fit 3rd/4th workflow in the 14‑day window.
- Only explain points to the client **if needed** (tradeoffs/add-ons).

## Point bands
### Small (3 pts)
Low edge cases, mostly single-system, low risk.
- Examples:
  - Inbox labeling/routing
  - Draft replies from templates
  - Simple scheduling handoff

### Medium (6 pts)
Two systems, approvals, templates, logging.
- Examples:
  - Leasing follow‑up + CRM updates
  - Maintenance intake triage + ticket creation

### Large (10 pts)
Multi-system, heavy branching, compliance/safety, or difficult data.
- Examples:
  - Turnover orchestration across vendors + PM tool + scheduling
  - Complex delinquency workflows with strict policy + audit trails

## Point multipliers (additive)
Add +1 to +4 based on:
- +2: strict compliance / high-risk comms
- +2: messy data / inconsistent inputs
- +1–2: multiple stakeholder approvals
- +1: needs dashboard/reporting layer

## Internal capacity target
Start with a **soft target of ~18–22 points per sprint** until we have 3+ real sprint baselines.

## Scoping guidance
- Always protect time for: monitoring, rollback, runbooks, training.
- If points exceed capacity, ship fewer workflows but **finish durable**.
