> **Template (internal).** Not legal advice. Have counsel review before using with clients.

---

## 1) Parties
This Data Processing Addendum (“DPA”) is entered into by and between:
- **Client (Controller):** **[CLIENT LEGAL NAME]**
- **Provider (Processor):** Icarus Foundry (“Provider”)

This DPA supplements the parties’ Master Services Agreement (“MSA”) and applicable Statement(s) of Work (“SOW”). In the event of conflict, this DPA governs with respect to Personal Data processing.

---

## 2) Definitions
- **Personal Data:** information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
- **Processing:** any operation performed on Personal Data (collecting, using, storing, disclosing, etc.).
- **Controller/Processor:** as understood under applicable privacy laws.

---

## 3) Scope and Roles
Client is the **Controller** of Personal Data processed under this DPA.
Provider is a **Processor** and will process Personal Data only:
- to provide the services described in the SOW(s), and
- in accordance with Client’s documented instructions.

---

## 4) Details of Processing
### 4.1 Categories of data subjects
- Residents/tenants
- Prospective residents/leads
- Owners
- Vendors/contractors
- Client employees/agents (as applicable)

### 4.2 Categories of Personal Data
May include (depending on workflow):
- Names, email addresses, phone numbers
- Property/unit identifiers
- Maintenance requests and related notes
- Appointment/scheduling details
- Communication content (emails/SMS)

### 4.3 Purpose of processing
To deliver automation workflows, drafting/triage, coordination, messaging, logging, and related operational improvements as described in the SOW.

### 4.4 Duration
For the term of the SOW/MSA plus any agreed retention period described in Section 9.

---

## 5) Client Instructions
Client instructs Provider to process Personal Data as necessary to:
- implement and operate agreed workflows,
- generate drafts/updates,
- integrate with Client systems,
- send messages only where explicitly authorized,
- provide monitoring/logging and handoff documentation.

If Client requests processing beyond the agreed scope, Provider may require written change order.

---

## 6) Confidentiality
Provider will ensure persons authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.

---

## 7) Security Measures
Provider will implement reasonable technical and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, such as:
- MFA on Provider-managed accounts where available
- Least-privilege access
- Access review on request
- Segregation of client data/workspaces where feasible
- Basic logging/monitoring for workflow failures

Client acknowledges that security also depends on Client’s environment and access controls.

---

## 8) Subprocessors
Client authorizes Provider to use subprocessors to deliver the services (e.g., hosting, analytics, AI model provider, messaging provider).

Provider will maintain a list of subprocessors used for Client services upon request.

Provider will remain responsible for its subprocessors’ performance of obligations substantially similar to this DPA.

---

## 9) Data Retention and Deletion
Upon termination or completion of services, Provider will, upon Client request:
- delete or return Personal Data in Provider’s possession, except to the extent retention is required by law or necessary for reasonable backup/archival practices.

Default retention (unless otherwise agreed): **[e.g., 30 days after termination]**.

---

## 10) Assistance
Provider will provide reasonable assistance (at Client’s cost if substantial) with:
- responding to data subject requests (access/deletion) to the extent applicable
- security questionnaires and basic documentation

---

## 11) Incident / Breach Notification
Provider will notify Client without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data security incident affecting Client Personal Data.

Notification window: **[e.g., 72 hours]**.

Provider will provide reasonable information about the incident, mitigation steps, and recommended actions.

---

## 12) Client Responsibilities (Consent + Messaging)
Client represents and warrants it has all rights and required consents to provide Personal Data to Provider and to communicate with recipients.

For **SMS** workflows, Client is responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable laws (including TCPA where applicable), consent management, and opt-out policy. Provider will implement opt-out handling to the extent supported by the selected messaging provider/workflow.

---

## 13) Liability
Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability in the MSA, unless otherwise required by applicable law.
