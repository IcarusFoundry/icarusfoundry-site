# Internal — Objection Handlers

## 1) “We need to think about it / talk internally”
**Goal:** identify the real blocker + set a concrete next step.

**Direct:**
“Totally fair. When teams say that, it’s usually one of three things: **budget, risk, or priority**. Which one is it for you?”

**If budget:**
“Got it. If we can tie this to a measurable win—hours saved, faster leasing response, fewer vacancy days—does that make it an easy yes? What number would make it feel like a no‑brainer?”

**If risk:**
“Understood. That’s why we default to **draft‑first** and graduate to auto‑send only for approved templates. Would you feel comfortable starting draft‑only for the first 14 days?”

**If priority/timing:**
“No problem. What’s the earliest start date that actually works? If we reserve a slot for [Date], can you confirm by [Day/Time]?”

**Close:**
“Let’s do this: I’ll send the recap + agreement today. Can we book a quick **10‑minute finalize call** tomorrow at [two options] to make the decision?”

---

## 2) “$7,500 is high / can you do less?”
**Goal:** defend value; trade scope/risk, not price.

**Value anchor:**
“I get it. The reason it’s $7,500 is we’re not selling ‘AI ideas’—we’re shipping **production workflows** in your systems with guardrails, monitoring, and training in 14 days. If it doesn’t stick operationally, it’s worthless.”

**ROI flip:**
“Quick question: what does one missed leasing lead cost you? Or one day of vacancy? If this saves even [X] hours/week or reduces vacancy by [Y] days, it pays for itself fast.”

**Trade scope, not price:**
“I’m open to making it fit, but I don’t discount by gut feel. The way we do it is: keep the price, adjust the scope/risk. We can do **draft‑first only** and focus on **two workflows** that hit the biggest ROI. That’s the fastest, safest path.”

**If they push hard for a discount:**
“If budget is the constraint, we can schedule for a later start date or reduce what we attempt in the 14 days—but I don’t want to take your money and under‑deliver.”

**Close:**
“Do you want to solve the problem, or do you want the cheapest option? If you want it solved, we’re the 14‑day execution team.”

---

## 3) Messaging add‑on objection (“Why $3,500?”)
“Because outbound resident messaging carries real risk. That $3,500 covers the extra controls: approved templates/categories, monitoring/logging, opt‑out handling, and a kill switch. Draft‑first is included; direct auto‑send is what we’re pricing.”
